Childhood Movies Showcase





WALL-E



GENRE: Sci-fi                   LENGTH: 1hr 38m                   RATING:


SYNOPSIS

WALL-E, short for Waste Allocation Load Lifter Earth-class, is the last robot left on Earth. He spends his days tidying up the planet, one piece of garbage at a time. But during 700 years, WALL-E has developed a personality, and he's more than a little lonely. Then he spots EVE, a sleek and shapely probe sent back to Earth on a scanning mission. Smitten WALL-E embarks on an adventure when he follows EVE across the galaxy.


Personal Opinion

This movie still makes me cry. The emotions put into this movie is insane, and even now it will forever remain as my all-time favorite movie. WALL-E is such a sweet robot, and he goes across the galaxy just to be with a robot he fell in love with. Because of that, he ends up saving all of mankind, and makes so many new friends, humans and robots alike. I aspire to be like WALL-E, and anyone who watches this movie should feel the same.







Surfs Up



GENRE: Family/Comedy                   LENGTH: 1hr 28m                   RATING:


SYNOPSIS

Surfing means everything to teenage penguin Cody Maverick (Shia LaBeouf). Followed by a documentary film crew, he leaves his home in Antarctica for Pen Gu Island, site of the the Big Z Memorial Surf Off. Cody wants to be respected and admired, and he believes that winning the competition will bring him what he craves.


Personal Opinion

Penguins surfing. And a chicken. If that's not the most amazing movie idea you've ever heard, I don't know what else to say. Growing up, I watched this movie every day (along with many others), and I never got tired of it. It's literally a hilarious documentary about a teenage penguin who enters a surfing competition, and his best friend is a surfing chicken. Cody finds love, discovers his hero is alive, and has the time of his life. Truly a 10/10 movie.







Cars 2



GENRE: Spy/Comedy                   LENGTH: 1hr 46m                   RATING:


SYNOPSIS

Racecar Lightning McQueen (Owen Wilson) and his tow-truck buddy, Mater (Larry the Cable Guy), have had their share of adventures together but nothing like what they experience when they head overseas for the first-ever World Grand Prix. While Lightning has his eye on the road to the championship, Mater takes a detour to international espionage when he meets British master spy Finn McMissile (Michael Caine) and stunning spy-in-training Holley Shiftwell (Emily Mortimer).


Personal Opinion

This movie was downright one of the coolest that came out when I was little, and it's still one of my favorite movies to this day. Now obviously, now that I'm older, I realize how insane it was that this movie was made for kids. There's definitely a lot of violence, and even a death scene for a spy car. But nevertheless, I still love it, and I'm pretty sure this movie is the reason I like action movies so much.
